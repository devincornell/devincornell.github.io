LLMs and other generative AI tools are now ubiquitous in information-related work. From composing emails to summarizing documents, there is no doubt that modern AI tools like ChatGPT have already had a big impact on individual productivity. What is less understood, however, is the advantage that these tools can have at the organizational level. Management consulting firms have been deemed ["early winners"](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/06/26/technology/ai-consultants.html) of the A.I. boom because organizations clearly want to invest - they're just not sure how. But do they have the technical chops to make it happen?

To really have an impact on business impact, I argue that we need to think about LLMs in terms of data infrastructure, rather than the Swiss army knife that it is for individuals. We need a shift from thinking of these as tools to thinking about them more as the connecting glue that can bring data systems such as document stores and data infrastructure.
